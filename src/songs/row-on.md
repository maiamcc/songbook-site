  Via Windborne, paraphrase: In 1964, Gale Huntington found the poem in the sailor's log and printed it in his collection, Songs the Whalemen Sang. A few decades later, English musician Tim Laycock set the poem (1980)


  Poem came from a novel about Arabella Stuart, 4th in line to the throne; married a nobleman who the king imprisoned out of fear of an attempt on the throne. In the novel, Arabella + lover correspond via letters that William's friend would toss to Arabella's maid as he casually rowed by on the Thames. In one scene, Arabella is worried that they are being watched, so the maid stands by the river and sings Row On as a coded warning message to William's friend that it is not safe to carry out their exchange of letters, and he should just "row on."
