---
title: Cairo Town
alternate_title: The Roustabout Song
author: Dillon Bustin
bop_rating: 3
structure: chorus
rnge: so-la
---
    I'm rolling, flowing, around these hills; 
    I must take my rest, but this river never will.
    Rolling, flowing, to Cairo Town,
    Just give me time to lay me down.

Boat's up the river, she won't come down.
I believe it in my soul, that she is water-bound.
Back her, slack her, bring her round,
Just give me time to lay me down.

Come, Rosianna, the boat is lying low,
Out on a sandy bar, way out in the Ohio.
Roustabouts are pulling, they're pulling mighty slow, Lord,
To give me time to lay me down.

I work on this steamboats for a dollar bill a day.
I buy a dress for Rosianne, and drink the rest away.
Captain, he just told me to call lead line today!
Lord, I got no time to lay me down.

All of the rich folks out on the promenade,
They twirl their parasols and they sip their lemonade.
I got hot steam to drink, I got a smokestack for my shade,
And I got no time to lay me down.

I load all of this freight by bale and by sack;
I tie it to the front and I lash it to the back.
A hundred eighty pounds of bale, a hundred ninety pounds of sack,
Two hundred pounds'll break your back.
