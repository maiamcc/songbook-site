	I am the chimera, it's fire I breathe;
	I do not dissemble, I do not deceive;
	All that I am is all that you see;
	"Unnatural" means nothing to me.

All across creation, my name it is known;
In pieces, in wholeness, my creatures are grown;
Between male and female my nature you'll find;
See, in every kingdom, my kind.

Listen in the forest, my voice in the trees;
The singing of the finches, the dancing of the bees;
Butterflies bi-colored, one sex on each wing;
The beauty and the strangeness I bring.

Athens and Rome threw my people in the sea;
In England, Victoria made science of me;
But to Ishtar, to Inanna, we were holy before;
Not man or woman, but more.

If my creatures, my children, can make you afraid,
Remember we all have been perfectly made;
Each sculpted whole by nature's own hand;
You fear what you don't understand.
